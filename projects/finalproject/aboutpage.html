Our Beginnings 🔙

El Parque de las Leyendas is the most influential, largest, and most visited zoo in Peru thanks to its more than 2070 animals and 215 different species, their entertainment areas, museums, botanical gardens, and different facilities. It was founded by the recognized conservationist Felipe Benavides on March 20, 1964 in the district of San Miguel in Lima. It was officialy named "Las Leyendas" but popular usage and the new administration of the Municipality of Lima changed the name to "Parque de las Leyendas" in the early 2000's. Our beatiful park started with no more than 30 animals in the 1970's until a zoo that already existed in the district of Barranco was permanently closed, and all the 400 animals that were part of this zoo were relocated to the Parque de las Leyendas facilities. Our park is proud to be home to animals from different parts of the world, taking into account the necessary care they require and ensuring their well-being.
